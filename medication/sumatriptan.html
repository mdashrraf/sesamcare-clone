FAQs

Frequently asked questions about sumatriptan

Medically reviewed by:Dr. Allison Edwards, MD
What is sumatriptan?

Sumatriptan is a prescription medication used to treat migraine headaches.

Migraines are severe headaches that are usually accompanied by nausea, sensitivity to sound and light, and numbness or tingling. According to the American Migraine Foundation, more than 36 million people in the U.S. suffer from migraines, which can sometimes be debilitating.

Whether youre passionate about going for hikes, cooking new recipes, or playing with your grandchildren, dont let migraines keep you from doing the things you love to do. Medications like sumatriptan can help control and manage your migraine symptoms - ensuring you spend less time recovering on the couch and more time enjoying the world.

What is sumatriptan used to treat?

Sumatriptan is used to treat migraines. It is generally used to treat acute migraines - those that come on suddenly - as opposed to chronic migraines. It is not used to treat other kinds of headaches, like cluster headaches.

How does sumatriptan work?

Sumatriptan belongs to a family of drugs known as selective serotonin receptor agonists. In simpler terms, these drugs narrow the blood vessels in your brain, preventing the brain from receiving pain signals and blocking the release of natural substances that cause pain, nausea, and many of the uncomfortable symptoms associated with migraines.

What are common side effects of sumatriptan?

While adverse reactions to sumatriptan are uncommon, some patients who take the drug may experience mild side effects, including paresthesia, pressure, chest pain, neck pain, vertigo, or malaise.

Rare, more serious adverse events have also been known to occur. If you are concerned that sumatriptan may be causing you to experience any number of adverse side effects, please contact your doctor or clinician.

This is not a complete list of side effects. For more information on adverse reactions you may experience while taking sumatriptan, please visit the National Institutes of Healths DailyMed webpage.

How long does it take for sumatriptan to work?

Sumatriptan can help quickly alleviate acute migraines. It usually takes 30 to 60 minutes to reach full strength.

What is the typical starting dose of sumatriptan?

Sumatriptan is usually taken in doses of 25, 50 or 100 mg. Larger doses are more effective but carry a greater risk of adverse reactions. If your migraine has not improved within 2 hours of taking sumatriptan, you may consider taking another dose. Please note, though, that your maximum daily dose of sumatriptan should not exceed 200 mg.

Talk to your doctor about the optimal dose to meet your needs.
